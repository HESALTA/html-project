History of Bantayan Church

Bantayan Church, formally known as the Sts. Peter and Paul Parish Church, was founded in the 16th century by Spanish missionaries. Over the years, it has stood as a beacon of faith, surviving numerous natural calamities and historical events, including the Spanish colonial period, World War II, and more. Its architecture reflects the fusion of Spanish and Filipino styles, making it an important part of the country's cultural heritage.

Services at Bantayan Church

The church hosts various religious services throughout the week, including daily Masses, special services during Holy Week, and regular prayer meetings. The church is known for its serene atmosphere, perfect for personal reflection or group prayers.

  • Daily Mass: 6:00 AM and 5:00 PM
  • Confessions: Available before or after Mass
  • Holy Week Services: Special services during Lent, culminating in the Easter Vigil
  • Wedding Services: Available with prior reservation

Significance of Bantayan Church

Bantayan Church is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of resilience and faith for the people of Bantayan Island. It serves as a spiritual home for the locals, offering a sense of community and support. The church is also a significant cultural landmark, often featured in local festivals and events that celebrate the island’s rich history and heritage.
